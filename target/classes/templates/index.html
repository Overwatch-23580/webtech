Who we really are &
why donate blood

As a blood donation non-profit organization, our mission is to ensure a safe and adequate supply of blood for those in need.

Donating blood is safe, easy, and quick.

Regular blood donation helps maintain a stable supply for patients.

Check eligibility

Aged between 18 and 65 years old, weigh at least 50 kgs, and be in good health. Some medical conditions or recent travel may also affect eligibility, so it's important to check with the donation center.

Some common questions
we're often asked

Who can donate blood?

In general, blood donors must meet certain eligibility criteria, which can include age, weight, and health status. Specific eligibility requirements can vary by country and donation center, so it's important to check with your local blood donation organization.

How often can I donate blood?

The frequency of blood donation can depend on factors such as your blood type, health status, and donation history. In general, most people can donate blood every 8 weeks or up to 6 times per year.

Is it safe to donate blood?

Yes, donating blood is generally considered safe for most people. Donation centers follow strict procedures to ensure the safety of both donors and recipients, and all blood donations are screened for infectious diseases.

What happens to my blood after I donate?

After you donate blood, it goes through a series of tests to screen for infectious diseases and determine your blood type. Once the blood is deemed safe for transfusion, it is typically separated into its individual components (such as red blood cells, plasma, and platelets) and stored until it is needed by a patient.

How to organize a blood donation in my community?

You can contact us to inquire about organizing a blood drive, which typically involves finding a suitable location, promoting the event, and recruiting donors.

What should I do after donating blood?

After donating blood, you should drink plenty of fluids, avoid strenuous activity for at least 24 hours, and follow any additional instructions provided by the donation center or medical staff.
